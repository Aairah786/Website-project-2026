What is accounting?

Accounting is the systematic process of recording, summarizing, analyzing, and reporting financial transactions. In simple terms, it is tracking how money moves. You learn how businesses make money, spend money, and how to use that data to make smart and helpful choices. It's a mix of logic, problem-solving, and business strategy. Without it, companies would not be able to evaluate if they are making a profit or falling into unmanageable debt.

What accountants do?

Accountants jobs are to manage, analyze, and report financial information to ensure accuracy, compliance, and informed decision-making for businesses individuals. They record transactions such as sales, purchases, receipts, and payments. They prepare vital statements like balance sheets, income statements, and cash flow reports to map out corporate health, help track taxes accurately, and guide growth strategies.

How does accounting promotes kaitiakitanga?

Accounting promotes kaitiakitanga (guardianship and care) by looking beyond just money. It focuses on taking care of our community financially and for future generations. Modern sustainability accounting ensures New Zealand businesses actively measure their environmental impacts, carbon footprints, resource conservation efforts, and ethical management alongside standard profits.
