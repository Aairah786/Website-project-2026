NCEA Academic Structure

Educational Pathways

A simple guide to picking your senior NCEA subjects and figuring out which university degrees to look at in New Zealand.

Senior High School Preparation

Year 11 Building Foundations

Start focusing on standard NCEA Level 1 Mathematics and English. Choosing commerce can help you build that base to guide you through accounting, developing communication skills and analysis.

Year 12 Intro to Business

Begin choosing NCEA Level 2 Accounting and Economics if your school offers them. This introduces basic ledger entries, financial statements, and macro-economic metrics, and how real-world businesses make decisions helping you gain the skills of an accountant.

Year 13 University Entrance

Give your full attention to NCEA Level 3 Accounting, Statistics (highly useful for data tracking over Calculus), and approved text-based subjects to guarantee University Entrance (UE) rank scores. This will lock in your spot at university.

Degrees & Qualifications

University Degrees

Once you finish High School, students usually pursue a Bachelor of Commerce (BCom) or a Bachelor of Business (BBus), majoring in Accounting. These three courses are great because they teach the foundational skills needed for a career in accounting, such as financial reporting, commercial law, taxation and auditing, and Data Analysis.

Professional Certification

To fully become a qualified professional, university graduates must complete an accredited program that is approved by CA ANZ. The accredited program is a university degree approve and checked by a professional organization like CA ANZ. It proves that the degree teaches the exact skills and laws you need to do the job safely and legally. After graduating, they complete three years of mentored work experience while finishing their professional accounting studies.
